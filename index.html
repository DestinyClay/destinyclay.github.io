About Me

My name is Destiny Clay. I am a Network Security Firewall Engineer and Cloud and Network Engineering student with a strong passion for enterprise networking and security.

I currently work in enterprise network security, supporting Palo Alto firewall environments across on-premises and cloud infrastructures in AWS and Azure. My experience includes firewall policy management, troubleshooting connectivity issues, validating network traffic flows, and supporting incident response efforts in production environments. I also work with internally developed tooling and platforms to manage firewall access requests and operational workflows.

In addition to firewall engineering, I have contributed to network security visibility and monitoring initiatives by developing an Azure Data Explorer dashboard used to track firewall throughput traffic and session counts. This work provided enhanced operational insight for the broader network security team and strengthened my experience with analytics and infrastructure monitoring.

I earned dual Associate degrees in Network Engineering and Network & Cybersecurity from Danville Community College and am currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Cloud and Network Engineering at Western Governors University. I also hold the Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA) certification, which strengthened my foundation in routing, switching, enterprise networking, and troubleshooting.

Beyond technical skills, I value leadership, collaboration, and continuous learning. During my time at Danville Community College, I served as President of the CyberKnights club, where I organized meetings, guided discussions, and supported fellow students throughout the program. I continue to seek opportunities to expand my knowledge through hands-on experience, operational problem solving, and ongoing professional development.

I believe in the power of continuous, lifelong learning, and I continue to actively seek out opportunities to grow my expertise through practical projects.
