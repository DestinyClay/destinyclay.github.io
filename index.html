About Me

My name is Destiny Clay. I am a dedicated Network Engineering and Cybersecurity student with a passion for technology.

With hands on experience with Cisco routers and switches, I thrive on tackling complex networking challenges and enhacing security measures. My technical skills encompass a wide variety of protocols and configurations, and I have successfully implemented solutions that have been adopted by my professor for future cohorts' networking projects.

As the current president of the Danville Community College CyberKnights club, I take the lead in organizing our monthly meetings. I create detailed agendas to keep discussions focused and ensure our professors stay on track. This leadership has fostered a productive environment where collaboration flourishes, and fellow students often turn to me for guidance and support in the program.

In addition to my networking expertise, I have hands on experience with Active Directory and virtual environments. I am proficient in configuring NTFS permissions, managing network shares, and organizing user groups. My experiece with virtualization platforms like Oracle VirtualBox, Proxmox, and VMware allows me to create and manage multiple virtual machines. These skills helped me enhance my understanding of network architecture.

My Cisco home lab along with the time I spend every week in my college's server room, is a cornerstone of my hands-on learning experience. This has allowed me to explore and troubleshoot networking concepts in a real world setting. I set up various Cisco devices to simulate complex network environments, where I diagnose and resolve issues, and implement configurations.

I believe in the power of continuous, lifelong learning, and I continue to actively seek out opportunities to grow my expertise through practical projects.
